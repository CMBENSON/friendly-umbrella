01 — Overview & vision

One pane of glass that stays calm under pressure

SRE teams live across a dozen tools. The signal that matters during an incident is scattered between Grafana, ServiceNow, Jira, the CMDB, an alert firehose, and a paging tool. O11Y Command pulls those streams into a single, opinionated workspace — built to be the tab that's always open, and the place you run an incident from.

The product is designed around two modes that share one shell: a steady-state mode for monitoring health, ownership and upcoming change; and an incident mode where the same data collapses into a war room. The visual language is deliberately understated — generous whitespace, soft surfaces, a restrained palette — so that the only things that shout are the things that are actually on fire.

Triage faster

Correlated alerts, blast radius and likely cause are one glance away — not five tabs.

See the whole system

Service health, dependencies and SLO budgets live in one connected model.

Close the loop

Copilot drafts the status update and the postmortem so the work doesn't end at recovery.

02 — Users & jobs

Built for the whole team, tuned for the person on call

The primary surface is a shared daily driver, but the hierarchy bends toward the on-call engineer mid-incident — the highest-stakes, lowest-patience moment.

On-call SRE

The default audience. Needs to know in seconds: what's broken, who's affected, what changed, and what to do next.

  • Acknowledge and triage from the alert inbox
  • Run the incident from the war room
  • Lean on the copilot for cause & next steps
Team lead / IC

Coordinates responders, owns comms, and signs off the postmortem.

  • Track responders, status and duration
  • Approve actions and publish updates
  • Review the auto-drafted RCA
Wider team

Engineers and managers checking system health, ownership, upcoming change and sprint work.

  • Glance at SLO budgets and degraded services
  • See who's on call and what's shipping
  • Track ServiceNow / Jira queues
03 — Information architecture

Seven destinations, one persistent shell

Navigation is grouped into Monitor (sense the system) and Operate (act on it). A persistent left rail carries live counts; the copilot is reachable from anywhere via the top bar; the RCA flow is summoned by the act of resolving.

Monitor

Overview · Alerts · Incidents

The pulse of production: KPIs and AI insights, the aggregated alert feed, and the incident war room.

Operate

Metrics & SLOs · Project board · On-call · Changes

The levers: Grafana panels and CMDB topology, the Jira sprint board, the rotation, and the change calendar.

Always-available surfaces

  • Copilot drawer — a grounded AI assistant, one click from the top bar on every screen.
  • RCA modal — the postmortem draft, triggered the moment an incident is resolved.
  • Tweaks panel — theme, accent and density controls.

05 — The AI layer

A copilot that knows the current state

AI is not a bolted-on chatbot — it's a connective layer with its own visual identity (a fixed violet, distinct from the tweakable accent) so it reads consistently wherever it appears. Every AI surface is grounded in the live state: incidents, alerts, degraded services, SLO budgets, in-flight changes and on-call are passed as context on each request.

Three AI surfaces, one identity

Proactive insights, the conversational copilot, and the auto-drafted RCA all share the violet treatment, the sparkle mark, and confidence framing — so users always know when they're reading a model's view versus raw telemetry.

Proactive insights

On the overview, four typed findings surface automatically, each with a confidence score, supporting evidence, and a one-click action:

  • Correlation — ties symptoms to a likely trigger (e.g. a recent change).
  • Prediction — error-budget burn-down and projected breach time.
  • Recommendation — a remediation grounded in similar past incidents.
  • Anomaly — a statistical deviation worth a separate look.

The copilot drawer

A slide-over assistant available on every screen. It opens with a situational greeting and suggested prompts, answers free-form questions in grounded prose, and can be invoked pre-filled from any insight's “Ask” affordance. Suggested actions are surfaced for review rather than executed silently.

Auto-drafted RCA

Resolving an incident summons a full postmortem draft — an AI-written executive summary over a deterministic spine (metadata, timeline, root cause, contributing factors, resolution, action items, lessons). From the draft, follow-ups push to Jira and the document publishes to Confluence in one step.

06 — Integrations map

What each system contributes

The product is a composition surface. Each integration owns a slice of the model and is always attributed in the UI.

Grafana

SLI / SLO panels and error budgets, war-room telemetry, and a primary alert source.

CMDB

Service topology and the dependency graph, plus service metadata — tier, owner, region, instances.

ServiceNow

INC / CHG / RITM / PRB queues, the change calendar, and incident alert signals.

Jira

Project & sprint work, incident-linked tickets, change releases, and RCA follow-ups.

Confluence

Publishing target for the postmortem / RCA document.

Alerts RSS

Aggregated webhook / RSS feed unifying Grafana, Prometheus, Datadog and CloudWatch.

On-call

Schedule, rotation, escalation policy and paging (PagerDuty-style).

08 — Layout & grid

A fixed rail, a fluid stage

The shell is a persistent 240px sidebar plus a fluid main column with a 60px top bar. Content lives in a scrolling stage built almost entirely from CSS grid with consistent gaps — never bare inline flow — so panels reflow predictably.

Shellgrid-template-columns: 240px 1fr — sidebar + main; top bar pinned, content scrolls.
KPI striprepeat(6, 1fr) — equal stat cards across the top.
Working area1fr 360px — a primary work column beside a fixed detail / context rail.
Insight & viz gridsrepeat(2–4, 1fr) — insights, SLOs, panels and service tiles.
SpacingOne --gap and one --pad token, both shifted by density.

Designed desktop-first for wide monitors — the natural home of a command center. Below ~900px the split columns and multi-up grids collapse to single column.

09 — Motion & states

Motion that informs, never decorates

  • Entrance — screens settle with a short translate; the visible state is the base, so content is never hidden if motion is suppressed.
  • Overlays — the copilot slides in from the right; the RCA modal scales up over a blurred backdrop.
  • Liveness — critical status dots pulse; the copilot shows a typing indicator while drafting.
  • Feedback — actions confirm in place (Acknowledge → state change; “Create Jira items” → “3 created”).
  • Respect — all motion honors prefers-reduced-motion.

Interaction states are explicit across the kit: hover lifts surfaces, selection draws an accent ring, disabled dims to ~40%, and empty states explain rather than show a blank panel.

10 — Theming & tweaks

Adaptable without losing its character

The whole surface is driven by CSS custom properties, so theming is a token swap rather than a rebuild. An in-product Tweaks panel exposes the safe knobs:

Theme

Light and a fully-considered dark mode for NOC walls and night shifts.

Accent

Five curated swatches; soft and ink tints derive automatically. The AI violet stays fixed.

Density

Comfortable or compact — shifts padding and gap tokens system-wide.

11 — Roadmap

Where it goes next

  • Copilot-suggested alert routing — dedupe and route across Grafana & Datadog automatically.
  • “Explain this chart” — an AI affordance on every Grafana panel.
  • NOC wall-display mode — a full-screen, auto-rotating view for shared screens.
  • Runbook execution — turn suggested actions into reviewed, audited one-click runbooks.
  • Live data binding — replace the mock layer with real Grafana / ServiceNow / CMDB connectors.
